The Feynman Technique

Teaching "The Diffusion of Innovation" as if to a child.

Imagine a small village where someone discovers a new type of grain that grows twice as fast. Some people try it immediately—we call them Innovators. Others wait to see if the village elders approve[1].

How would you explain why the "Wait-and-See" group takes so long to change their ways?

I think it's because they are afraid of the risk. If the grain fails, they won't have food for the winter. They need to see social proof from their neighbors first.

Spot on. That risk-aversion creates a "chasm" in the adoption curve[2]. You're describing the Early Majority perfectly.

Core Framework

Diffusion of Innovation (Everett Rogers) explores how ideas spread. The five stages of adoption are:

  • 1. Knowledge: Exposure to the innovation.
  • 2. Persuasion: Forming a favorable attitude.
  • 3. Decision: Crucial junction. High risk-aversion leads to stagnation here.
  • 4. Implementation: Putting it to use.
  • 5. Confirmation: Final reinforcement.
